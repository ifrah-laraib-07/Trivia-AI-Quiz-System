INTRODUCTION
------------
Biochemistry is the study of chemical processes
occurring within and relating to living organisms.
It bridges biology and chemistry.

BIOMOLECULES
------------
1. PROTEINS
   Made of amino acids. Perform most cell functions.
   Examples: enzymes, antibodies, structural proteins.

2. CARBOHYDRATES
   Primary energy source. Made of C, H, O.
   Glucose = C6H12O6. Stored as glycogen in animals.

3. LIPIDS
   Fats, oils, waxes. Energy storage, membrane structure.
   Hydrophobic - do not dissolve in water.

4. NUCLEIC ACIDS
   DNA and RNA. Store and transmit genetic information.
   Monomers are nucleotides.

ENZYMES
-------
Biological catalysts that speed up chemical reactions.
Each enzyme is specific to one substrate (lock and key).
Temperature and pH affect enzyme activity.
