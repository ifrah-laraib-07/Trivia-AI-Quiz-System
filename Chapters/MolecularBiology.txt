INTRODUCTION
------------
Molecular Biology studies the molecular basis of
biological activity, especially DNA, RNA, and proteins.

CENTRAL DOGMA
-------------
DNA -> RNA -> Protein
1. Transcription: DNA is copied into mRNA in nucleus.
2. Translation: mRNA is read by ribosome to make protein.

DNA REPLICATION
---------------
- Occurs before cell division.
- Helicase unwinds the double helix.
- DNA Polymerase adds complementary bases.
- Results in two identical daughter DNA strands.

MUTATIONS
---------
A mutation is a change in the DNA sequence.
Types: Point mutation, Insertion, Deletion, Inversion.
Mutations can be harmless, harmful, or beneficial.

GENE EXPRESSION
---------------
Not all genes are active at all times.
Gene regulation controls which proteins are made.
Promoters and repressors control gene expression.
