INTRODUCTION
------------
Genetics is the scientific study of genes, heredity,
and variation in living organisms. It explains how
traits are passed from parents to offspring.

KEY TERMS
---------
DNA  - Deoxyribonucleic acid, the hereditary molecule.
Gene - A segment of DNA that codes for a protein.
Allele - Different forms of the same gene.
Genome - The complete set of genes of an organism.

MENDEL'S LAWS
-------------
1. Law of Segregation: Each trait is controlled by a
   pair of alleles that separate during gamete formation.

2. Law of Independent Assortment: Genes for different
   traits are inherited independently of each other.

DNA STRUCTURE
-------------
- Double helix shape, discovered by Watson and Crick.
- Made of nucleotides: Adenine, Thymine, Guanine, Cytosine.
- A pairs with T, G pairs with C.
- Stores and transmits genetic information.
